FAQ

Questions Answered.

Is Inside the Grind NCAA / NIL compliant?
+
Yes. Inside the Grind is designed around the NCAA's NIL rules. Athletes monetize their own personal brand content, not athletic performance — which falls squarely within NIL guidelines. We recommend athletes consult their compliance officer, but the model is built to be compliant.
How much do athletes actually earn?
+
Athletes keep 80% of every subscription. At $4.99/month per fan, that's roughly $4.00 per subscriber per month. 100 subscribers = ~$400/month. 500 subscribers = ~$2,000/month. Growth depends on consistency, authenticity, and engagement.
What kind of content should athletes post?
+
Anything authentic — game-day prep, practice reflections, mental health moments, diet and recovery routines, goals and setbacks. The more real and consistent you are, the more subscribers stay and grow. Highlight reels are for social media. This is for your real story.
Can fans cancel their subscription anytime?
+
Yes, absolutely. Fans can cancel at any time with no penalty. Subscriptions run month-to-month. If a fan cancels mid-month, they retain access until the end of the current billing period.
Is this only for football players?
+
Not at all. Inside the Grind is for all College, High School & Pro athletes — football, basketball, soccer, track, swimming, wrestling, volleyball, and more. Any athlete with a real story to tell is welcome.
How do fans find athletes to subscribe to?
+
Fans can browse athlete profiles by sport, school, or popularity. Athletes with active public posts are more discoverable, which is why we encourage a mix of free and subscriber-only content to maximize reach and conversions.
Do athletes have to post every day?
+
There's no requirement, but the platform is built around consistency being rewarded. Athletes who post regularly tend to retain subscribers better and attract new ones faster. We recommend 3-5 posts per week during season, 2-3 in offseason.
